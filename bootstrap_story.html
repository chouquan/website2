Making London the world’s best big city for cycling ！

The Mayor's Transport Strategy, published in March 2018, sets out a vision for a fairer, greener, healthier and more prosperous London. This includes making cycling appealing to many more people, and ensuring that London's streets are safe and accessible for everyone who wants to cycle.

The cycling boom under the coronavirus pandemic!

Data released by Transport for London shows that since the began of the coronavirus pandemic, there has been a significant increase in walking and cycling activities in the UK. After the first national lockdown in March 2020, people's travel habits have changed significantly.



Beside, facing traffic congestion in big cities, environmental pollution caused by automobile exhaust, and the further expansion of urban planning , bicycles are undoubtedly a beneficial solution. Bicycles have become a trend again. This may be another choice for us to enter the future.

Current Chanllenge

Currently, unsafe is the biggest obstacle that prevents public cycling in London. According to the Attitudes to Cycling survey (2017) ,More than half of people do not ride bicycles because they are afraid of traffic accidents.

Although there has been a significant decrease in the risk of being killed or seriously injured while cycling in London since 2000, there is a high risk when travelling by bicycle (Vision Zero action plan, TFL). In 2019, 9892 people died or were seriously injured in bicycle accidents in London. There were 1682 serious accidents.

How safe is cycling in London?

We hope to answer four questions:

Where did the cycling accident happen?

Where are the infrastructures that help improve cycling safety？

What are the characteristics of the spatial distribution of cycling accidents, cycling infrastructures and cycling commuter flow?

How should each district adjust its policies to provide a safer riding environment?

Spatial Scale and Data Source

This research focused on the middle layer super output area(MSOA) of London (983 districts). The MSOA was created by the Office for National Statistics and used to explore the small areas of the city. It can make it easier for us to find problems in a smaller area.


The data we used in the research include： cycling infrastructures data (Transport for London), cycling accidents data (Department for Transport),cycling commuter flow (PCT).

Research Method

We obtained 4 types of related data and performed data merge, log transformation and standardized processing for different analysis needs.

First, use spatial autocorrelation analysis to observe the spatial distribution of casualties, identify areas with high casualties cluster based on the Moran I and LISA analysis. Then visualize the distribution of casualties in this area with the road network as the smallest geographic unit, which visually display the number of casualties in each street.

Next, the variables were chosen to cluster based on the MSOA level in London in order to explore the current status and challenges of cycling safety from the perspectives of cyclists and managers, which is conducive to the development of targeted policies to improve bicycle safety

Where do casualties happen?

According to statistics, in 2019, the total number of casualties was 9,892, and they mainly occurred in the centre of London. The number of casualties in the central area was significantly higher than in the outer areas of the city. Westminster, Southwark, Lambeth, Tower Hamlets and Wandsworth were the five districts with the most casualties.

Cluster:Low Risk But Cycling Inactive

Destribution of Cluster:Low Risk But Cycling Inactive

This cluster consists of suburban areas in outer London. The MSOAs have the lowest casualties in cycling accidents while they have the least cycling infrastructure.


Residents are less likely to commute by cycling as both of the average origin and destination ratio are lower than 2%. Cycling in the MSOAs is relatively safe but the infrastructures like parking lots and bicycle lanes could be hard to find.

Cluster:High Risk and Cycling Active

Destribution of Cluster:High Risk and Cycling Active

This cluster is mainly made up of areas in Central London. Although the MSOAs have the most cycling infrastructures, they could not prevent cycling accidents taking place in the areas. On the contrary, these MSOAs are at the highest risk of cycling.


The possible explanation could be that the areas are densely populated and residents tend to commute by cycling. The tourist attractions like parks and museums that are densely located in the areas also encourage tourists to ride. Beside cycling infrastructures, other ways such as traffic control should be considered to improve cycling safety.

Cluster:Low-medium Risk and More Crossings

Destribution of Cluster:Low-medium Risk and More Crossings

The cluster is characterized by the largest average number in crossings. Compared to other clusters, the risk of cycling is quite low and other infrastructures are at average level.


To improve cycling safety in the MSOAs, efforts should be made to coordinate the flows of bicycles, motor vehicles and pedestrians at crossings by setting up other infrastructures like signals and advanced stop lines, for instance.

Cluster:Low-medium Risk and More Traffic Calming

Destribution of Cluster:Low-medium Risk and More Traffic Calming

This cluster shares most of the characteristics with the green cluster except for the number of crossing and traffic calming. Most of the MSOAs are located in Outer London and are safe for cycling.


In addition, they have the second largest average number of traffic calming, indicating a large number of residences or complexity in terrain.

Cluster:Medium Risk and Cycling Dependent

Destribution of Cluster:Medium Risk and Cycling Dependent

This cluster is notable for the large proportion of people who ride bicycles to commute and the second largest number of parking lots. However, other infrastructures are not corresponding to the high demand of cycling, especially the density of cycling lane and the number of signals.


Most of these areas are located around Central London and have more casualties in cycling accidents than the outskirts. Setting up more cycling infrastructures would have more potential in improving cycling safety than other parts of the city.
