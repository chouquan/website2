Making London the world’s best big city for cycling ！

The Mayor's Transport Strategy, published in March 2018, sets out a vision for a fairer, greener, healthier and more prosperous London. This includes making cycling appealing to many more people, and ensuring that London's streets are safe and accessible for everyone who wants to cycle.

The cycling boom under the coronavirus pandemic!

Data released by Transport for London shows that since the began of the coronavirus pandemic, there has been a significant increase in walking and cycling activities in the UK. After the first national lockdown in March 2020, people's travel habits have changed significantly, from 29 percent between January and March 2020 to an estimated 46 percent between April to June 2020 (Travel in London 2020).

Beside, facing traffic congestion in big cities, environmental pollution caused by automobile exhaust, and the further expansion of urban planning (housing is getting farther and farther from subway stations), bicycles are undoubtedly a beneficial solution. Bicycles have become a trend again. This may be another choice for us to enter the future.

Current Chanllenge

Currently,unsafe is the biggest obstacle that prevents public cycling in London. According to the Attitudes to Cycling survey(2017),more than half of Londoners who don't cycle choose not to because they are scared of traffic.

Although there has been a significant decrease in the risk of being killed or seriously injured while cycling in London since 2000, there is a high risk when travelling by bicycle (Vision Zero action plan, TFL). In 2019, 9892 people died and were seriously injured in bicycle accidents in London. There were 1682 serious accidents.

Where do Casualties happen?

According to statistics, in 2019, the total number of casualties was 9,892, and they mainly occurred in the centre of London. The number of casualties in the central area was significantly higher than in the outer areas of the city. Westminster, Southwark, Lambeth, Tower Hamlets and Wandsworth were the five districts with the most casualties.

The spatial distribution of Infrastructures

There are 87,903 facilities that are conducive to safe riding. Southwark, Hackney, Lambeth, Lewisham and Ealing are the five districts with the largest number of infrastructers.
